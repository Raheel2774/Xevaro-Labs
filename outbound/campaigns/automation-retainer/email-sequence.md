# Cold Email Sequence — "Never Miss a Lead" (reply-focused, NO links)

**Goal:** get interested roofers to **reply "yes."** That reply = a warm lead you call/close. We do NOT sell or link in the cold email — the email's only job is to start a conversation.

**Target:** trades that HAVE a weak/outdated website (so emails are scrapeable — roofers/contractors ~50%+ yield). Use the email machine for these; no-website trades go on the call list.

**Rules:** plain text, **no links, no images** (best deliverability + reply rate). One idea per email. CAN-SPAM footer required (real sender name + physical mailing address + opt-out). Send from a secondary domain at scale; ~10–15/day max from a personal Gmail while testing.

---

## Email 1 — Day 0 (the hook → ask for a reply)

**Subject:** quick question about [Business]
**Subject alt:** missed calls at [Business]?

> Hi [FirstName],
>
> Quick question — when someone calls [Business] and you're tied up on a job, what happens to that call?
>
> For most [roofers/plumbers] I talk to, the caller just hangs up and dials the next name on Google. That's a paid job, gone.
>
> I set up a simple system that auto-texts every missed call back in ~5 seconds, so you catch those leads instead of handing them to a competitor.
>
> Worth a look? Just reply "yes" and I'll send over a 2-minute breakdown.
>
> [Your Name] — Xevaro Labs
> [physical mailing address] · reply STOP to opt out

## When they reply (this is the whole point)

| They say | You do |
|---|---|
| "yes" / "sure" / "send it" | Send a 2-min Loom or text breakdown, then: **"Want me to walk you through it on a quick 15-min call? Thursday or Friday?"** — book the call. |
| "how much?" | "$997/mo, and it usually pays for itself with one saved job. Quick call so I can show you the ROI for your numbers — when works?" |
| "not interested" | "No worries — out of curiosity, how do you handle calls you miss right now?" (often re-opens) |
| any positive signal | **Book the call. Don't try to close over email — the call is where you win.** |
